Optimisation of Electrical Infrastructure

Optimal design of Underground Residential Distribution from a single CAD input

The scope is to reduce the construction costs of electrical networks. The scope is limited to greenfield underground residential developments on the Ausgrid network. This required the model to optimally choose kiosk substation locations. This is completed using Quadratic Programming (QP). The project also has to produce Minimum Spanning Trees for both LV and 11kV networks. Prim's Algorithm was used to optimise the spanning trees. The model is imported and exported in the form of CAD drawing files. The electrical infrastructure designs produced has to meet all Ausgrid network standards.

Key Findings are a construction cost reduction of 51.1% when compared to existing electrical networks. This is a multi-million dollar savings for large residential developments. The number of substations required was reduced by 46.62% when compared to existing networks. This increases efficiency and reduces environmental impacts. This project also automates the electrical infrastructure design process.
